What is Money?

Money is something everyone accepts in exchange for goods and services. For example, when you buy a toy or food, you use money to pay for it. It can be coins, notes, or digital forms.

Why Do We Use Money?

Money helps us trade things more easily than bartering. Instead of exchanging a chicken for eggs, we use money as a common medium that everyone trusts.

Value of Money

Meaning: The value of money is its purchasing power—how much goods or services it can buy.

Changes Over Time: It varies with inflation (value falls as prices rise) and deflation (value rises as prices fall).

Relative Measure: Money’s value depends on what it can purchase, not on a fixed amount.

Market Forces: Controlled by supply and demand—more demand or less supply raises value, and vice versa.

Purpose: Must hold value to work as a medium of exchange and a store of value.

Key Points

Inflation decreases purchasing power.
Deflation increases purchasing power.
Value depends on market forces.

TIME VALUE OF MONEY and Why it is Important in Financial Management

⏳
