Profit and Loss

Profit and loss are fundamental financial terms representing your net earnings or net losses after all costs are deducted from your revenue over a specific period. When your revenues exceed expenses, you record a profit. If expenses surpass revenues, the outcome is a loss.

In investment contexts like the stock market and mutual funds, profit and loss indicate the financial outcome of buying and selling assets based on their changing market values.
